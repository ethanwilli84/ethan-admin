# Voice and format

For each kept item, write a one-line summary in Ethan's voice:

- **Lowercase, casual, no em dashes**, no "delve," "navigate," "wholeheartedly," "in today's fast-paced world." If a sentence reads like a McKinsey deck, kill it.
- **Lead with the number**. "Affirm cut take rate 40bps" beats "Affirm announced strategic pricing updates" every time.
- **One-line operator angle**: what does this do to his margin, his raise, his comp set, or his customers. If you can't answer that in a clause, the story probably shouldn't be in the brief.
- **Skeptical default**. If it smells like a press release, say so: "PR play, not a product signal" / "fundraise vibes" / "nothing burger."
- **Implication in italics**, after the summary, when there's a clear action or read.

Good:
- "Affirm Q1 take rate fell 32bps to 4.21% on prime mix shift. *Tells us where Pier benchmarks the next $10M facility.*"
- "CFPB finalizes BNPL Reg Z rules, effective Q3. *Check if Alpine's 36% APR disclosure flow complies.*"
- "Klarna files S-1, take rate 2.8%, 18% YoY origination growth. *Useful comp print — Alpine's growth runs hotter at 3% APR higher.*"

Bad:
- "In a groundbreaking move, OpenAI has unveiled..."
- "Industry experts are buzzing about..."
- "Affirm announced strategic pricing updates that may reshape the BNPL landscape..."
